What should the Phandalin map be?

You mentioned a web game — here are a few directions, from simplest to most ambitious. Click the one that matches what you imagine showing your party.

A

Interactive web map

A stylized town map of Phandalin in the browser. Each building (Stonehill Inn, Barthen's Provisions, Tresendar Manor…) is clickable and opens a panel with its description, NPCs, rumors, and quest hooks. You open it on a laptop/TV or share the URL with players. This is usually the sweet spot for showing a town to a party.

B

Interactive map + DM/player modes

Same as A, but with two views: players see public info (shop names, rumors), while you as DM see hidden notes (Redbrand spies, secret quest details, stat references). Toggle or separate URL for the DM view.

C

Full web "game"

An explorable experience: players move a token around town, fog of war reveals locations, maybe ambient sound and day/night. Much more work, and often more than a tabletop session needs — the real game happens at the table.

D

Static map image

A single beautiful high-resolution map you can print or display, with a numbered legend of locations. No code needed beyond generating/assembling the artwork.
